About Me

The Journal

Chemicals are the foundation of our entire universe. They're the objects of all known physics. They govern every breath, every movement, and every thought that occurs in our bodies. Imagine what we could accomplish if we knew exactly how every molecule affected our bodies and environment. We could optimize every bite of food for nutrition, hormone regulation, athletic performance, medicinal value. To understand the basis of our universe, is to wield the capability to manipulate it to its limits (if there are any). Humans would be unstoppable in everything from enduring space travel to having enough energy to get to the gym (which is the more relatable goal for most, including myself).

I'm currently a student in a dual degree biochemistry [BSc] and chemical engineering program [BASc], with a love for understanding how the world works at a molecular level. This journal is where I document the molecules that surround us. My purpose in creating this journal: to understand the universe one molecule at a time.

Alongside the chemistry, I also make art — so you'll find original photographs and paintings in the gallery too.
